About Me

A builder
who also
breaks things.

"I build systems and then make sure they hold."

I am a backend developer and QA engineer. Two disciplines that, in my mind, have always belonged together. My journey started with a degree in International Relations, which taught me to think in systems, spot edge cases, and communicate clearly across contexts. Those turned out to be exactly the skills both roles demand.

On the backend side, I work with Python, Java, and JavaScript to build and maintain reliable server-side logic including APIs, data pipelines, messaging systems, and the databases that underpin them across both SQL and NoSQL. I am comfortable diving deep into how a system is wired together, not just how it behaves on the surface.

On the QA side, I bring that same depth. Writing automated test suites with Selenium, validating APIs with Insomnia, spinning up containerised test environments with Docker, and integrating quality gates directly into CI/CD pipelines. I am evolving from manual testing to owning full automation frameworks alongside the engineering team.

What drives me is the challenge of making complex systems simple, reliable, and user-friendly. Whether that means writing the code, testing it, or building the infrastructure that keeps both honest.
